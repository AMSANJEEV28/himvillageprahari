Initiative Overview

Between 13 December and 26 December 2025, Him Village ePrahari Foundation conducted Thermocol (EPS) Awareness Sessions in collaboration with Shrunkhala Organisation for Sustainable Development.

These sessions were held at Government Primary School, Gangdaspur, Bahadrabad, Haridwar, focusing on environmental literacy, health awareness, and responsible waste management among young students.

Why Thermocol Awareness?

Thermocol (Expanded Polystyrene) is commonly used in packaging, decorations, and disposable products. While lightweight and convenient, it poses serious environmental, health, and wildlife risks when mismanaged.

Environmental Impact

Non-biodegradable material that persists in soil and water bodies, including the Ganga, for hundreds of years.

ü´Å Health Hazards

Burning Thermocol releases toxic gases such as Carbon Monoxide and Styrene vapours causing respiratory and neurological damage.

Wildlife Threat

Animals often ingest Thermocol mistaking it for food, leading to choking, poisoning, and death.

Important Clarification:
Thermocol is 100% recyclable when collected, segregated, and sent to specialized recycling facilities. The problem is not the material ‚Äî it is irresponsible disposal.
